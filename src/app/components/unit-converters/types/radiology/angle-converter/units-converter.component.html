Angle Converter

The Angle Converter is a versatile tool that allows you to convert angle measurements from one unit to another. It's an invaluable resource for various fields, including mathematics, physics, engineering, and navigation.

Using the Angle Converter is straightforward. Follow these steps to accurately convert angles:

  1. Enter the value you want to convert in the input field. This value represents the angle you wish to convert.
  2. Select the unit you are converting from in the "From" dropdown list. This specifies the initial unit of angle measurement.
  3. Select the unit you want to convert to in the "To" dropdown list. This designates the desired angle unit for the conversion.
  4. The converted value will be displayed in the result field, providing you with an accurate angle conversion result.

For example, if you need to convert degrees (°) to radians (rad), enter the value in degrees, select "Degree" as the "From" unit, and "Radian" as the "To" unit. Click "Convert," and you will see the equivalent angle measurement in radians.

The Angle Converter supports a wide range of angle units, including degrees, radians, minutes of arc, and seconds of arc, making it an indispensable tool for professionals, students, and enthusiasts who require precise angle conversions for their tasks and projects.

Degree (°) Angular Unit

The degree (°) is a unit of angular measurement commonly used in geometry, geography, and various scientific disciplines. One degree is defined as 1/360th of a complete circle, and it is subdivided into minutes and seconds for more precise measurements.

Radian (rad) Angular Unit

The radian (rad) is a unit of angular measurement used in mathematics, physics, and engineering. It represents the angle subtended at the center of a circle by an arc with a length equal to the circle's radius. Radians are often preferred in trigonometric and calculus calculations.

Conversion: 1 Radian (rad) = 0.0166667 Degrees (°) = 60 Arc-Minutes (') = 3600 Arc-Seconds ('')

Radians are the standard unit for angular measurement in many scientific and mathematical contexts.

Sextant Angular Unit

The sextant is an angular unit that represents one-sixtieth (1/60) of a full circle or revolution. It is commonly used in navigation, particularly for measuring the altitude of celestial objects above the horizon.

Conversion: 1 Sextant = 60 Degrees (°) = 1/6 Quadrant = 1/360 Revolution = 1/360 Circle

Sextants are essential tools for celestial navigation at sea and in other applications where precise angle measurements are required.
